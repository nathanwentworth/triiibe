"Artist’s collective Triiibe illuminates play of individuality and belonging"

March 28, 2016

"The artists’ collective Triiibe has a rare conceit: Three of the central members are identical triplets. Alicia, Kelly, and Sara Casilio, now in their 30s, started out as guerrilla performance artists back when they were students at Massachusetts College of Art and Design. They were eye-catching anyway, walking together down the street. Add concepts and costumes, and you had performance art."

February 2, 2016

"The Fitchburg Art Museum opened their newest exhibit: Triibe: Same Difference this past Sunday, February 7th. Triplets Alicia, Kelly, and Sara Casilio teamed up with National Geographic photographer Cary Wolinsky in the creation of this exhibit. Triibe: Same Difference will hit home for everyone as it focuses on the social, political, and equality opportunity issues in our today’s world. The exhibit is challenging, thought provoking, interesting and completely relevant."

'TRIIIBE' - 3 sisters, one photographer and multiple concepts at Fitchburg Art Musuem

February 28, 2016

"Boston-based performance artists Alicia, Kelly and Sara Casilio, who are identical triplet sisters, and National Geographic photographer Cary Wolinsky have worked together since 2006 as TRIIIBE, an artistic collective whose meticulously staged photographs, performances and videos play with our concepts of identity, cultural and gender issues, driving home their point engagingly - and in triplicate. They are first and foremost performance artists but the group has translated living theater into a lively photographs that are at once accessible and challenging, humorous and poignant."
Read more at The Telegram
