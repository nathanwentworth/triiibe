About the Exhibit

TRIIIBE: same difference will be on display at the Fitchburg Art Museum from February 7th to June 5th 2016. This group of performance artist’s work reflects on various social and political issues, almost every piece of art comprising of several conceivable messages. This exhibit is definitely one you should bring your thinking cap to!

The exhibit begins in the mezzanine, providing a sneak peek to the expression of their art. The room is filled with actual get-ups TRIIIBE used in their photos. This exhibit is shown rooms dedicated to their projects. Their most recent project is one shown in what is called “the tryptic room”. The tryptics have only been on display once before, this time much more intimately. The room successfully complements the feel of the project, serene and ecclesiastical.

In addition to the tryptic room, TRIIIBE’s exhibit displays their visual commentary art in its own white-walled, wooden-floored room so that there is no distraction to the audience and their reading of the photos. Unlike many other art exhibits, TRIIIBE’s also includes a room exposing their works in progress and a video room with some behind the scenes footage.

Whether alone or with company, it is guaranteed you will go home feeling inspired. TRIIIBE’s art influences you to view things from a different perspective, so prepare to leave the show in a different state of mind than the one you arrived with. This exhibit is powerful, intriguing, and an impressive way of exposing visual commentary on tons of relatable topics.
